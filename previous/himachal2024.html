Trip to Himachal

Shimla - Kasol - Manali

6 Nights 6 Days

Day 0: Start from Delhi at Night

This is the day you should start from Delhi. Delhi acts as perfect base point for all himalayan plans. Popular bus services are there from Delhi to Shimla, Manali and so on. The buses typically cost 1600-2400 INR and are all semi-sleepers. We started at 11 pm at night for Shimla.

Day 1: Shimla - The Queen of Hills

Reached Shimla at 8 am. Headed back to hotel and went out to visit some popular places nearby. And spent the most amazing evening roaming around none other than the Shimla Mall Road.

Mall Road: A bustling street lined with shops, cafes, and colonial-style buildings, offering great views of the surrounding hills.

Day 2: Let's Go to Kasol

We spent the morning roaming around Shimla ki galiyan. The morning fog, the subzero temperature made the queen of hills glow on a different level. After that we had a long jounrye ahead of around 7 hours to Kasol. The path includes coming down from the Shivalik range to catch the Beas river and travel through this valley to Bhuntar and take a right to offroad to Kasol or Manikaran. We started at 9 am. and stopped for lunch in between and reached Kasol at 4 pm. Kasol is a village with Parvati river in the valley surrounded by lower himalayan ranges. This place is famous for Himachal weed, Rave parties by Israelis, local markets and ofcourse its amazing beauty.

Mall Road: A bustling street lined with shops, cafes, and colonial-style buildings, offering great views of the surrounding hills.

Day 3: On the Lap of Malana - Kasol

Including little adventurous incidents we spent the day enjoying Kasauli. Had amazing local foods, went to shopping, rave party. Most importantly, we spent the day on Kasol riverside cafes soaking in the natural bliss. The day has seen snowfall all around the valley surrounding mountains.

Mall Road: A bustling street lined with shops, cafes, and colonial-style buildings, offering great views of the surrounding hills.

Day 4: Manali Chalein?

It started snowing all around Himachal. So we started around 11 am. Usually it takes 3 hours to reach Manali from Kasol. However due to heavy traffic and snow, we reached Manali around 6 pm. Took rest and enjoyed mall road of Manali.

Mall Road: A bustling street lined with shops, cafes, and colonial-style buildings, offering great views of the surrounding hills.

Day 5: Snowy Times

This was the most epic day scheduled in our itinirerary. We planned for Solang Valley and Gulaba on this day. A day packed in white world. We started around 7.30 in the morning, which is crucial for travelling in snow conditions. The roads get slippery and causes heavy traffic otherwise. We took some more jumpsuits on the way and reached Solang around 8.45 in the morning. Just after crossing a pool, the car couldn't move further due to lack of traction and it was around 3 kms away from the valley. So we chose to walk. Slow walks by the snowy roads and sideways was more than just beauty. Soon reached the valley, and the day was worth it. We did skiing, zipline, played like 9 year olds and didn't look back at all from there. Had around 8 cups of tea that day to keep ourselves warm. We got down at 4 pm at the car location. Usually it should take 1 hour to get back but the traffic made it 3.5 hrs. Anyways, a day to remember always.

Mall Road: A bustling street lined with shops, cafes, and colonial-style buildings, offering great views of the surrounding hills.

Day 6: What else?

This was our last day in Manali. So, we visited some local spots, did some shopping, roamed around mall road. I found an amazing eatery just outside the Manali town and on the opposite side of Beas river. So we headed to that 8 kms away eatery. It was nice cafe on the lap of the lower himalayn ranges. Open air and trees made it even more pleasant. Along with the cafe Rati's Homemade was there. Rati, a lady of ladet 60s grows berries and other fruits in her backyard and sells achaar, jam and much more from it. The essence and ambience of the place was amazing. You can check it out here. Later that night we had our bus from Manali bus stand to Delhi.

Mall Road: A bustling street lined with shops, cafes, and colonial-style buildings, offering great views of the surrounding hills.

Day 7: See You on the Next One

Reached Delhi 8 am in the morning. We still spent a day in Delhi but that was not necessary. You can head back home right away.
